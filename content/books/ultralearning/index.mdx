# Ultralearning — Summary (Preview)

Scott Young's *Ultralearning* distills a decade of self-directed learning experiments into a playbook for mastering hard skills on your own.

## The Core Idea

Most learning fails because it is too easy, too broad, or too unfocused. **Ultralearning** is the opposite:

- **Intense** — full-time, all-in projects
- **Self-directed** — no one else is setting the curriculum
- **Concrete** — measurable outcomes, not vague "improvement"

## The Nine Principles (Preview)

1. **Metalearning** — Research the subject before you start
2. **Focus** — Cut out distractions; direct your energy
3. **Directness** — Practice the actual thing you want to be able to do
4. **Drill** — Attack your weakest points
5. **Retrieval** — Test yourself, don't re-read
6. **Feedback** — Find honest signal on your performance
7. **Retention** — Schedule review over time
8. **Intensity** — Do more in less time
9. **Resilience** — Push through the hard parts

## Why It Works

Traditional education optimizes for breadth and credentialing. Ultralearning optimizes for **skill acquisition per hour of effort**. By stacking the principles above, you can compress years of conventional learning into months.

> "The point of ultralearning isn't to make learning easy. It's to make learning *effective* — and worth the effort."

## When to Use It

- You need a new skill for a career pivot
- You want to add a hard skill to your existing toolkit
- You're between jobs and have focused time
- You want to prove to yourself you can do hard things

## When *Not* to Use It

- You're already deep in a high-performance role (the opportunity cost is too high)
- The skill doesn't actually matter for your goals
- You haven't done the metalearning step yet (you'll waste time)
