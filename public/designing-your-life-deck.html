Life as a
Design Problem

Start Where You Are Chapter 1

The Life Dashboard

Rate your current levels of Health, Work, Play, and Love. You can't design a path forward without knowing where you are.

01 Health Your physical, emotional, and mental well-being. Rate from 0–Full
02 Work Productive activities — paid or unpaid. Rate from 0–Full
03 Play Pure joy activities with no outcome pressure. Rate from 0–Full
04 Love Family, friends, partners, and community. Rate from 0–Full

Gravity Problems: If you can't change it, accept it and design around it.

Navigate using real-time clues: track your engagement and energy across daily activities for three weeks.

Getting Unstuck Chapter 4

Mind Mapping
& The Mashup

Generate a flood of ideas by building a three-tier associative mind map, then mash distant concepts together.

01 Pick an Anchor Start with a positive activity from your Good Time Journal.
02 Associate Freely 5–6 radiating arms, then secondary and tertiary layers.
03 The Mashup Pick 3 distant concepts → combine into one actionable idea.
"I am never truly stuck. I can always generate fresh options by breaking down my assumptions."

Design Your Lives Chapter 5

Three Odyssey
Plans

Map out three completely different 5-year futures — each with a 6-word title, 3 key questions, and a dashboard rating.

Life 1 Optimized Path The direct extension of what you're doing now — your clearest current trajectory. Resources · Likability
Life 2 Alternative Route What you'd do if Life 1's industry or role vanished overnight. Confidence · Coherence
Life 3 Unconstrained Dream What you'd pursue if money and expectations were removed. Wild Possibility

Career Landscape Ch. 7–8

The Hidden
Job Market

Online job postings are a tiny fraction of open roles — and they're designed to screen you out. Build human connections instead.

The Trap

Mass-applying to job boards treats you as a data point. Algorithmic filters screen you out before a human reads your name.

The Way In

Network activation → prototype conversations → uncover latent needs → pitch tailored solutions.

"Your dream job is co-created, not found — built through relationships that uncover unadvertised needs."

Resilience Ch. 9–10

Choosing Happiness
& Failure Immunity

Good life design relies on confident decision-making and treating failure as data, not shame.

4-Step Decision Process

1. Gather & create options
2. Narrow to 3–5 choices
3. Combine rational + somatic signals
4. Let go and move on

Failure Reframe Exercise

1. Log the failure
2. Categorize: screwup / weakness / growth opportunity
3. Distill the core insight

Community Chapter 11

Building a
Life Design Team

You cannot design a well-lived life alone. The best lives are co-created with an active ecosystem.

Core Team Trusted Peers Meet regularly to review Odyssey Plans, hold you accountable, and offer honest feedback. 3–5 People
Mentors Wisdom Guides Further along the path. They share stories, spot blind spots — never impose values. Diverse Perspectives
Community Affinity Groups Broad networks where you share resources, swap ideas, and discover prototype opportunities. Expand Your Reach
